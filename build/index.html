Tracking Police Killings in the U.S. Since 2015

Using data collected by the Washington Post, we generated two infographics concerning the number of people that have been fatally shot by the police from 2015-2021. We’ve used a map-based scatter plot and bar graphs to visually show the amount of shootings as well as the circumstances of each shooting, breaking them down by state. the purpose of our endeavour is to give you a better understanding of what the are the circumstances truly like when police fatally kill civilians.

Interesting Perspectives

POINT 1

Number of shootings. Although the US is known to have issues with police killing, thousands of people have been killed in the past 7 years. This is notable comparing it to the number of police killings in other nations. The U.S. has one of the highest percentages when it comes to police killings per 10 million people.

POINT 2

Variety in data. Often when these shootings make headlines, the profile of the victim paints these shootings as if they are just a problem that affects one demographic. The unfortunate robustness of our data shows this is an issue which affects every age group and demographic.

POINT 3

Spread of victims location wise. The number of police killings in the country are more spread out than what one might believe, considering the preconceived notions people have of some US states, particularly cities, being hubs for police violence and murder.

POINT 4

The majority of these victims did not show any signs of mental illness which also helps to combat ideas of these victims as “crazy” or “insane”.
